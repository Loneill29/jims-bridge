SPREADING THE JOY


Through the work of our dedicated volunteers and contributions from our generous donors, Jim's Bridge has been able to make a difference in the lives of children in need in the Chicagoland area. Below are highlights from some of our contributions over the past few years.


CAMP BUTTERNUT SPRINGS

Even when school is not in session, Jim’s Bridge finds ways to help less fortunate children. This year, we were delighted to help fund a wonderful summer camp program sponsored by the Erie Neighborhood House. About 95 children, ages 5-12 attended summer camp at Camp Butternut Springs near Valparaiso, IN. This four day event included canoeing (many had never seen a canoe before), archery, arts & craft, fishing, frog hunting, capped by movies at night! Each child wore their personally tie-dyed shirt on the journey home. And they already want to go back next year! Click here to watch the video.

ST. GALL SCHOLARSHIP AWARDEES

Directors Maureen Mikulski and John Mikulski met with all scholarship awardees on June 5, 2017 to talk with them about their many accomplishments during the 2016-2017 school year as well as their plans for relaxation during the summer vacation.

VISITATION SCHOOL LEADERSHIP AWARDS

On May 25, 2017, Directors Maureen Mikulski and John Mikulski attended the awards ceremony which included its 21 eighth grade graduates. During the ceremony, they presented three Leadership Award scholarships for the 2017-18 School Year at Visitation School.

ERIE HOUSE HOLIDAYS

The Erie House’s 2016 Holidays with Heart celebration provided over 600 gifts to children and families in need within the Erie House community. In addition to sponsoring six children, Directors Maureen Mikulski, Kathy Mikulski, and John Mikulski as well as Chris Diaz participated in the Erie House Holidays with a Heart Program Gift Wrapping on the evening of December 9, 2016.

VISITATION CHRISTMAS FESTIVAL

On December 9, 2016, Directors Maureen Mikulski and John Mikulski as well as Jim’s Aunt and Uncle volunteered their time to assist at the Visitation School in their annual Christmas Festival. They helped the school children with shopping for holiday gifts for their family and friends.

CELEBRATION OF GIVING

The holidays can be a difficult time when you are unable to afford warm clothes or a small toy for the little ones. The mission of the Celebration of Giving is to provide children and families served by Catholic Charities and those in need with gifts and necessities during the holiday season. Celebration of Giving will touch the lives and bring joy to many families who may not have been able to celebrate the holidays. During their 2015 event, through the collection of donations from individuals, corporations and organizations, Catholic Charities distributed gifts and toys to more than 25,000 children and families. The James F. Mikulski Memorial Group was proud to sponsor a family of grade school age and younger children through the Celebration of Giving program.

BACKPACK DONATION

On August 16, 2016, Directors Maureen, Kathy, and John Mikulski distributed backpacks filled with school supplies that were donated by Jim’s Bridge to the pre-schoolers at the Erie House who are heading to Kindergarten for the 2016-17 School Year.

VISITATION LEADERSHIP SCHOLARSHIPS

On May 26, 2016, Directors Maureen Mikulski and Kathy Mikulski presented two Leadership Award scholarships for the 2016-17 School Year at Visitation School.

On March 17, 2016, Jim’s Aunt & Uncle as well as Directors Maureen Mikulski & John Mikulski volunteered their time to assist as judges at the Visitation School Science Fair. They helped rate the science projects for the school children in Grades 5 through 8.

CELEBRATION OF GIVING

Now in its 68th year, Catholic Charities annual Celebration of Giving is a concerted effort to collect holiday gifts for children and families served by Catholic Charities’ wide array of programs. It touches the lives and brings joy to many families who may not have been able to celebrate the holidays. In 2014, through the collections of donations from individuals, corporations, and organizations, Catholic Charities distributed gift and toys to more than 25,000 children and families. The James F. Mikulski Memorial Group was proud to sponsor a family of four grade school age and younger children through the program.
